Verifiable Audit Trail

A complete, tamper-evident record of every operation — protecting your business and your aircraft

What Gets Logged

Every significant event in your hangar is automatically captured with a precise timestamp and operator context

Aircraft Movements

Every tug command, path taken, start position, and final placement is recorded — including which operator authorized the move.

Collision Warnings

All proximity alerts and collision warnings are timestamped with aircraft identities, location in the hangar, and how the event was resolved.

Operator Actions

Staff logins, command authorizations, manual overrides, and system configuration changes are all tied to individual user accounts.

System Health Events

Camera connectivity, tug battery levels, sensor calibration checks, and any system faults are continuously logged for maintenance records.

Why It Matters

Insurance protection: In the event of a ground incident, a complete verifiable record of system state, warnings issued, and operator actions provides objective evidence of due diligence — protecting your FBO from liability.

Regulatory compliance: Maintain documentable records for airport authority requirements and insurance audits without any manual paperwork burden on your staff.

Staff accountability: Tie every action to a named operator. Reinforce safe practices and quickly identify the root cause of any incident during a review.

Incident investigation: Replay any event sequence with timestamps and positional data to understand exactly what happened — and prevent it from happening again.

Continuous improvement: Aggregate log data reveals usage patterns, peak congestion times, and recurring near-misses that inform smarter hangar layout and scheduling decisions.

How It Works

The audit trail runs automatically in the background — no operator action required. Every event is written to a secure, append-only log the moment it occurs. Logs are stored locally and synced to the cloud, ensuring records are preserved even in the event of hardware failure.

Authorized personnel can search, filter, and export logs directly from the tablet interface, generating formatted reports for specific aircraft, date ranges, or event types in seconds.
